Why do this?

Lending someting you own to anyone on the internet is odd - I agree, but I still think the idea comes from a good place.

The logic goes a little bit like this:

  1. I own this thing.
  2. I use it maybe a few times a year.
  3. I don't want to sell it.
  4. I don't want to throw it away.
  5. I don't want to give it away.
  6. …but I still don't need it for let's say 361 days a year.

I know it might get broken/stolen/lost but I think I'm OK with the risk.

Plus, it seems like the right thing to do.

How does this work?

First of all we will agree when you can borrow it.

The easiest thing is if we meet up; pick any café near Stoke Newington (London, UK).

If you are miles away, then we can use the post - but you will have to cover the cost of postage there, and back to me when you are done.

And that's basically it, get in touch, borrow my bike tool and fix your bike!

Questions?

Here are the questions I have answers to, if you want to know something else then get in touch.

  • What's the tool? : It's a chain whip, for changing the cassette (gears/cogs/sprocket) on the bikes back wheel.
  • How do I use a chain whip? : Watch this video - https://youtu.be/7-MYHOpzT5A.
  • Does it fit any make/bike? : The chain whip is standard, but this lockring tool is for Schimano cassettes.
  • Do you have product details? : Yes, from amazon: Chain whip, and Lockring tool.
  • Will I need anything else to use it? : Yes, you will need a spanner, torque wrench or socket wrench to fit the lockring tool.
  • Can I collect it from your house? : No, we can meet in a café near Stoke Newington (London, UK).
  • Will you post it? : Yes, if you pay for postage and also send it back when you are done.
  • Will you add other tools? : I guess it depends if anyone borrows this one.
  • How long can I borrow it for? : As long as you need, but this is usually a quick fix.
  • What if it breaks? : That's OK, these things happen. If you feel bad you could buy me a new one and keep the project going!
  • Does it cost nothing? : Yes, you borrow for no cost. You can donate if you want to support the project, but that's optional, and I haven't figured out how to do that yet.
  • Why are you doing this? : It seems like the right thing to do, and it's interesting to see if people would borrow instead of buy.
  • Can I add my own tool to this site? : No, but if you're keen to join in that's awesome, send me an email.
  • What if I hurt myself, or break my bike? : I hope that doesn't happen, but from a legal point of view I don't take responsibility for anything you do with these tools.
  • What's your goal? : It would be cool to help someone fix their bike.

Legaly; I take no responsibility for anything.
